O Canada

Thanksgiving 2017

2017-11-23

I wake up cocooned in milky sheets, a splurge purchase celebrating the move into a new apartment.
Should I get out of bed? No, it’s my day off… but I have work to do.
I jolt upright and embrace the chill of Winter that taps at the door. Tap, tap, tap. Slippers greet wood floor.
I need to buy groceries. What’s on the list? Penne, butter, parmesan, broccoli…. But first, coffee.
I crawl into my favourite crook of the living room. Outside, skyscrapers dance in the clouds, in muffled pomp and circumstance from festivities three streets down.
I’m thankful…

For my parents, immigrants to a land of freezing temperatures and unfamiliars, but whose sweat and struggles were met with open doors and opportunities.
For growing up in a community that accepted me not because of gender or colour or weight or accent but because I am.
For a space to express my opinions without fear, and friends who encouraged me to reach for dreams unreachable.
For teachers who educated, doctors who healed and cured, and police who protected.
For a government made up of as many women as men, that champions women and girls, and men and boys too.
For a country built on the backbone of First Nations whom history oppressed, providing the foundation to a nation that welcomes all, those unwanted, forgotten, and marginalized.

It’s true what they say. You don’t know what you have till it’s you’re gone. I’m a Canadian living in America and this Thanksgiving, I’m thankful to be Canadian.
